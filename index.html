Favorite Sports Team

My Favorite NBA Team

I have been a fan of the lakers since 1996. It was Kobe Bryant's rookie year drafted from high school. The Lakers had also traded for Orlando Magic's star Shaquille O'neal. The Lakers are one of the most successful and popular professional franchises in all American sports. The franchise has won a combined 17 Basketball Association of America (BAA) and National Basketball Association (NBA) titles, tied in league history with their rival the Boston Celtics.

My Favorite NBA Players

MJ and Kobe

Micheal Jordan is one of the best basketball players ever. MJ led the Chicago Bulls to six NBA Championships and earned the NBA's MVP Award five times and six Finals MVP awards. Kobe Bryant is also known as one of the best basketball players ever. He led the Los Angeles Lakers to five NBA Championships. He earned one NBA's MVP Award and two Finals MVP awards.

Shaq

Shaquille O'Neal is one of the most dominant players in NBA history. He helped lead the Los Angeles Lakers to three NBA Championships. He also helped lead the Miami Heat to a NBA Championship. He was a double-double machine. He was practically unstoppable as he scored at will on his opponents.

My Favorite NFL Players

Peyton Manning

Peyton Manning is one of the most prolific passers in NFL history. He won an NFL-record five MVP awards, as well as two Super Bowl championships. He won a championship with the Indianapolis Colts and the Denver Broncos.

Terrell Owens

Terrell Owens played in the NFL for 16 seasons. He is a six-time Pro Bowl selection and five-time first-team All-Pro. He ranks third in career receiving yards at 15,934 and third in receiving touchdowns at 153.

My Favorite College Team

Arkansas Razorbacks

The Arkansas Razorbacks are in one of the toughest conferences known as South Eastern conference. They aren't as prominent as some of the big name schools but they have been able to compete with the best. The Razorbacks have been ranked as high as #2 in the nation under the leadership of then head coach Bobby Petrino.
